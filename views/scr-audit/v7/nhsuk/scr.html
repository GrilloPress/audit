Your Summary Care Record is a short summary of your GP medical records. It tells other health and care staff who care for you about the medicines you take and your allergies.

Your Summary Care Record comes from your GP record. It tells health and care staff:

  • your allergies
  • your medicines
  • any reactions you've had to medicines

Health and care staff might look at your Summary Care Record:

  • in an emergency
  • when you're on holiday
  • when your surgery is closed
  • at out-patient clinics
  • when you visit a pharmacy

Staff will ask your permission to look at your Summary Care Record. Except from in an emergency, for example where you are unconscious and need urgent care.

Only staff with the right levels of security clearance can look at your Summary Care Record.
