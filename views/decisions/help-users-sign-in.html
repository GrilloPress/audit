When a user's session expires, or they need to authenticate, and they are still intending to be active we should tell them why they need to sign back in.

The system should not redirect them to the log in page without notice.

Once reauthenticated the system should:

  • redirect them from the page they were previously

    • or

  • continue from the action they indended to take before they were asked to reauthenticate

Examples

  • Page users see if their session has ended

Hypotheses

If we give users a page that tells them why they need to log back in, and how,
then users will know what to do next if they need to reauthenticate

We know this works when:

  • users successfully continue their journey even after being asked to log back in

If we redirect users back to where they were before they had to reauthenticate
then users can continue using the service how they intended

We know this works when:

  • users successfully continue their journey even after being asked to log back in
  • users don't return to the first page of the service after reauthentication
