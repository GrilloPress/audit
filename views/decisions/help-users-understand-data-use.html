When a user starts a service that shows or audits their health records their records will be processed.

This is implicit in the service, but a user may not be aware exact how and why their data is being used.

The system should:

  • clearly state what data is being used
  • why that data is used
  • what data is shared or processed elsewhere

Examples

  • Page users see before they audit their Summary Care Record

Hypotheses

If we use plain language to explain what data will be used and why
then user's will know what information they are happy to have processed

We know this works when:

  • users report increased confidence in the NHS's ethical management of their data (in terms of this service)
  • users reports higher usability
  • users are able to explain (at a high-level) what information is being processed and why
