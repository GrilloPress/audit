The NHS number is a 10 digit number, like 485 777 3456.

It must always be displayed with a space after the first three digits. With another space after the next three.

Issues with the NHS number

The NHS number is not a well-known identifier. Even within the NHS.

Users scanning the page will often mistake it for their National insurance number.

Avoid asking for it, or provide another way to complete the task without it.

Strategies

  1. Be clear that it is needed

    Tell users upfront and often that the NHS number is needed by using something like an interruption card

  2. Provide another way to succeed

    Give users a choice to use something other than their NHS number, like their postcode

  3. Provide clear guidance when you ask for it

    Tell users the format of the NHS number and provide an example.

  4. Tell users where to find it

    If you haven't introduced the NHS previously, tell users where to find it before asking for it.
